Martin Scorsese

Martin Scorsese is universally revered as one of the most influential and seminal auteurs in the history of cinema, a master filmmaker whose prolific career is defined by his profound exploration of themes like guilt, redemption, violence, and Catholic identity, often through the lens of complex, morally ambiguous characters. From his gritty early classics like "Mean Streets" and "Taxi Driver" to epic crime sagas such as "Goodfellas", "Casino", and "The Departed", he has crafted an indelible portrait of American life, often focusing on the underworld and the frayed edges of society. Beyond gangster films, his versatile genius spans a vast range of genres, including the spiritual crisis of "Silence", the dark comedy of "The Wolf of Wall Street", and the lyrical grandeur of "Hugo".
